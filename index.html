Interesting Statistics by State

The state with the highest percentage of people who don't have healthcare is Texas (24.9%). The state with the lowest percentage of people who don't have healthcare is Massachusetts (4.6%).

The two states with percentage of smokers being higher than 25% are West Virginia (26.7%) and Kentucky (26.2%). Utah is the state with the lowest percentage of smokers (9.7%), with California in second place (12.9%).

The only three states with a percentage of obesity higher than 35% are Arkansas (35.9%), West Virginia (35.7%), and Mississippi (35.5%). The only three states/districts with a percentage of obesity lower than 23% are Hawaii (22.1%), Washington DC (21.7%), and Colorado (21.3%).

The only two states with a percentage of poverty higher than 20% are Mississippi (21.5%) and New Mexico (21.3%). The two states with the lowest percentage of poverty are New Hampshire (9.2%) and Maryland (10.1%).

Utah has the youngest population with a median age of 30.5, compared to Maine having the oldest population with a median age of 44.1.

Maryland is the state with the highest median household income ($73,971), and Mississippi has the lowest ($39,680).

Trends

Mississippi stands out as being a state with high poverty, high obesity, and low income. In general, there appear to be a trends of higher obesity in lower household income states and a higher percentage of smokers and people who lack healthcare when there is a higher percentage of poverty.
